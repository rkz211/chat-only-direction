# The Concept Gap

### A formal theory of failure, repair, and human necessity in language-model software systems

*Roark Pinkerton (RKZ). Formal companion to the experience report "Directing Software Through Conversation" and the narrative "A Brief History of Thornwood." This document is structured for machine ingestion and adversarial review: definitions precede use, claims are numbered and cross-referenced, derivations name their dependencies, and §7 lists falsifiers. Prefer attacking a numbered claim over the whole.*

**Status:** theory derived from a single production system (Thornwood, ~35.6k-line hybrid deterministic/LLM engine; **1,610 commits** git-measured through 2026-07-23, of which **~1,200 were bug-fixes** — commits ≠ fixes; the rest are features/content/refactors). n=1. Predictive, not proven. See §8.

---

## 0. Definitions

- **D1 — Concept.** A determinate meaning: a referent with fixed structure and truth-conditions, independent of its symbol. *(e.g. the traffic rule HALT-AT-THE-LINE.)*
- **D2 — Language / symbol.** A token or string that *points at* concepts. The pointing relation is **many-to-many** and **context-dependent**: one symbol maps to a set of candidate concepts; the intended one is selected by context. *(e.g. "stop" → {HALT, CEASE-FORWARD, DESIST, …}.)*
- **D3 — Concept-space vs language-space.** *Concept-space* = decisions made by a fixed symbol→meaning binding (e.g. deterministic code: the symbol IS the operation). *Language-space* = decisions made by re-deriving the intended concept from a symbol at use-time (e.g. an LLM interpreting a prompt).
- **D4 — LLM.** A model of the statistics of language-space: given context, a distribution over plausible symbol-uses/continuations. It operates in language-space (D3). It does **not** hold concepts (D1) as stable objects; it reconstructs a concept from symbols at each use.
- **D5 — Decision node.** A point in a computation where a symbol must be resolved to the concept it denotes in context (a word interpreted, an instruction applied, a reference dereferenced).
- **D6 — Slip.** At a node (D5), selection of a concept from the candidate set (D2) other than the context-intended one. A slip is *locally plausible* (the chosen concept is in the candidate set) and may be *globally fatal*.
- **D7 — Trajectory.** An ordered sequence of decision nodes constituting one run/task.
- **D8 — Invariant meaning.** The single context-intended concept for a symbol, held fixed across a trajectory (D7).
- **D9 — Delta.** For an agent stuck at a node, the minimal information that, added to what the agent already holds, resolves the node correctly: the conditional content *H(intended concept | agent's current state)*.

## 1. Axioms

- **A1 (non-identity).** Language ≠ concepts. The symbol→concept map (D2) is not injective and not context-free. *A symbol underdetermines its concept.*
- **A2 (high correlation).** The map is nonetheless strongly correlated with concept-structure (language evolved to carry concepts). Over random contexts, the modal symbol-use denotes the intended concept with high probability.
- **A3 (substrate).** An LLM (D4) has access only to language-space (D3); it has no direct access to concept-space. All its outputs are selections in language-space.
- **A4 (determinism of code).** A deterministic program binds each symbol to exactly one operation at every execution (a concept-space decision, D3). Its slip-probability *on the re-interpretation class* (P1-type: choosing a different concept for the same symbol at run-time) is **0 by construction** — the binding does not vary per execution, so there is nothing to "read one house over." This is *not* a claim that code is failure-free: it can encode the **wrong** binding (a wrong spec), miscompile, or hit hardware faults — a residual ε. But that ε is a **different, non-compounding class**: a one-time authoring error, fixed once and not re-rolled at every node (contrast P2's per-node re-selection). The asymmetry A4 asserts is narrow and true: code cannot commit the *runtime concept re-selection* that P1 names.
- **A5 (concept-holding director as the decorrelated reader).** A person who has *directed the build of the system* holds its invariant meaning (D8) and design intent, and can diff a symbol's use at any node against it — with errors **decorrelated from the acting LLM's on the semantic class** (no same-distribution LLM has this decorrelation; a fresh outside expert lacks the held invariant). The qualifying property is authorship/direction of *this* system, installed by the directing; the profile is a competent AI-literate IT professional, not a unique talent. *(Empirical, load-bearing, replication-critical; an existence proof — demonstrated n=1, generalization plausible-not-proven. Not an infallibility claim. See P9 reformulation and §8.)*

**Corollary A2/A1 (the working-but-fallible duality).** From A2, an LLM is usually correct (capability). From A1, it is not always correct (fallibility). **Capability and fallibility are two faces of one fact — the correlation's strength and its imperfection** — so capability gains and residual fallibility tend to move together; a *purely capability-side* intervention (a bigger model) is not expected to separate them. (This is a tendency argued from the correlation axiom, not a no-exceptions law.)

## 2. The failure theory

- **P1 — Pointwise divergence (the slip is the atomic failure).** *From A1, A3, D6.* Because a symbol underdetermines its concept (A1) and an LLM selects in language-space (A3), at any node the selection may be a locally-plausible non-intended concept (D6). LLM failure is therefore not (primarily) global incompetence but **local**: correct across a complex trajectory, wrong at an isolated node. *Illustration: an agent applies compound rules correctly, then reads "stop" as CEASE-FORWARD rather than HALT and turns — a defensible reading, a fatal act.*

- **P2 — Reliability does not scale to certainty via capability, at unbounded complexity (the union bound).** *From P1, A2.* Let a trajectory (D7) have `N` decision nodes with per-node slip probabilities `p_i`. Run failure requires ≥1 fatal slip; under rough independence the clean-run probability is `∏(1 − p_i) ≤ (1 − p̄)^N`. **The qualitative bound does not need independence:** `P(≥1 fatal slip) ≥ max_i p_i^{fatal} > 0`, and positive correlation among slips only *raises* the joint failure probability — so the result is robust, not contingent on the product formula. Increasing capability lowers each `p_i` but never to 0 on the semantic class (A1: structural, not a defect). **Scope honestly:** for a *fixed* `N`, capability that lowers every `p_i` does drive `P(fail) → 0` — the formula says so. The load-bearing regime is the real one, where **task complexity grows `N` as fast as (or faster than) capability shrinks `p_i`**: there `P(run fails)` is bounded below by a quantity capability alone cannot zero. So the honest slogan is not "reliability is independent of intelligence" but **"the reliability of an *unboundedly-complex* task is governed by node count and jurisdiction, not by intelligence alone."** *Corollary: "use a smarter model" is a constant-factor reduction of a risk that grows with `N`, not a solution to the failure class.*

  > **Remark (the load-bearing premise is the weak one).** All downstream results (P3–P11) require only the *observable* premise: **`p_i > 0` and slips compound over `N`.** They do **not** require the strong metaphysical reading of A3 — that the model has *no* internal concept-space access and is *only* a language-statistics engine. That strong reading is an *explanatory intuition* for *why* `p_i > 0`; it is contested, and current interpretability evidence for concept-like internal representations cuts against it. (Note A1 itself — polysemy / symbol underdetermination — is *not* the contested part; it is ordinary linguistics. The weight the skeptic contests sits on A3-strong.) Whether a slip is a concept that is absent or a present concept momentarily mis-selected is immaterial to P2 onward. A skeptic may reject A3-strong and retain the entire theory; it is therefore demoted to a heuristic, and the theory is anchored on the arithmetic. *(See also §8, degree-vs-kind.)*

- **P3 — Two orthogonal failure modes.** *From P1 + observation of repair.*
  - **P3a — Depth failure (slip):** the *originating* reasoning fails at one node by choosing the wrong concept (P1). Failure of *precision*.
  - **P3b — Breadth failure (overreach):** the *repairing* reasoning, searching the space of valid fixes for a defect, selects a globally-valid but disproportionately broad fix (a fix whose scope exceeds the defect's scope). Failure of *proportion*. *Illustration: "car too heavy for engine" → "eject Earth's iron to lower gravity" (valid causal chain, planetary blast radius) vs "lighten the chassis."*
  - P3a and P3b are orthogonal: one is a wrong point, the other is a too-large region. Different cures (§3).

## 3. The repair theory

- **P4 — Correction non-convergence (the guard-wall is economic, not an impossibility).** *From P1.* **Core claim (A3-strong-independent):** a guard added for slip `s` catches the observed **instance** ("here, `stop` = halt") but leaves the symbol's ambiguity intact everywhere else it recurs (the **class**); each guard is itself language (P1) with its own slip-edges; so the guard set **grows without bound** and its instance-coverage never overtakes the instance-supply. **The failure is asymptotic/economic, not logical** — no finite fixed point closes the class. This core needs only "guards are language and close instances, not classes"; it does *not* need any claim about the model's inner life. **Scope discipline (why humans/review *are* corrected by language):** a correction converges when it lands somewhere the **class can be closed** — a concept-holder who generalizes the fix, or a deterministic binding/checker that forecloses it (P5). Whether an LLM can *also* be a class-closing recipient (via activation-steering / concept-editing / class-targeted fine-tuning) is an **open empirical question** — the interpretability evidence (Remark after P2) leaves it open, so P4 does *not* assert the LLM categorically cannot; it asserts only that *external guard-stacking* around the decision does not converge. *Motivates — does not alone prove — P5; P5's terminating property is what distinguishes it.*

- **P5 — Repair by moving the decision *or its acceptance test* into concept-space.** *From A4, D3, P4.* A terminating cure removes a slip **class**, not an instance, by putting a **concept-space** (deterministic, non-re-interpreting) authority in the loop. Two architectures do this, and both are terminating:
  - **(a) Migrate the decision** — replace symbol-interpretation with a deterministic binding (A4); the node leaves the LLM's jurisdiction entirely (its P1-slip probability → 0 by construction). "Remove the ability to lie" = "remove the ability to re-interpret."
  - **(b) Check the output** — leave the LLM deciding but gate its output with a deterministic **acceptance test** (constrained/grammar decoding, a type-checker, a legal-move generator, proof-carrying output). The slip class ("emits an illegal/ill-typed/unproven result") is foreclosed without migrating the decision.
  
  **Both require the class be DECIDABLE** — you can write the binding *or* the checker. That is the real boundary: on decidable classes a terminating concept-space cure exists (a/b); on **undecidable semantic-judgment classes** neither is writable, and the residue is exactly where the human concept-holder (P9) is irreducible. *Per P2, total risk falls by node-**removal / node-foreclosure**, not node-improvement; monotone and terminating (unlike P4). (Corrects an earlier overclaim that jurisdiction transfer was the "only" terminating cure — output-checking is a second one on the same decidable domain.)*

- **P6 — The repair-agent must be reach-bounded (proportionality), and reasoning-effort is non-monotone for repair.** *From P3b.*
  - **P6a (proportionality):** the fix's admissible scope must be constrained to the defect's scope, else P3b dominates. Structural enforcement (module isolation) beats exhortation: an agent confined to a module *cannot* emit a cross-module fix.
  - **P6b (inverted-U in effort — observed, fragile):** for the *repair* task, expected damage *appeared* non-monotone in reasoning effort. Below a floor, the agent could not isolate the fault (failed); above a threshold, added reasoning *tended to* widen the searched solution space and P3b-type broad fixes came to dominate; a middle band did best. *This would invert the standard "escalate when stuck" heuristic (monotone for building) for repair.* **Confound to control before trusting it:** breadth-of-fix is an outcome, and the effect is at least as consistent with "high effort *under unconstrained prompting* overreaches" — i.e. an artifact of missing **P6a** (reach-bounding), not of effort itself. F-P6b is only runnable once "effort" is operationalized (reasoning tokens? tier? time?) *and* P6a is held fixed. Treat as the most fragile proposition after A5.

- **P7 — Throughput bound and its cure.** *Observation + P6a.* Once the repair agent is reach-bounded (P6), correctness is handled and the binding constraint becomes **wall-clock**: each observe→fix→verify cycle is LLM-latency-bound and compounds over the defect tail; latency does not fall with capability. Cure = **parallelism**, whose precondition is **module isolation** — the *same* structure that enforces P6a (bounded reach) also enables concurrent, collision-free agents (bounded latency). Isolation is thus doubly load-bearing. *Consequence (jurisdiction of modularity): modules are needed for the debug phase, not the build phase — the builder tolerates an interwoven monolith; the debugger fleet cannot. Modularity's classical justification (human comprehension) is replaced by (reach-bounding + concurrency).*

  > **Remark (P7 refinement — prep/act/verify decomposition; BEING IMPLEMENTED 2026-07-23, no metrics yet — folded into PAPER Appendix D as in-flight).** Within the observe→fix→verify cycle, only the *fix* (code mutation) is irreducibly serial, and only **per module**, for two reasons: write-collision, and **attributability** (one change in flight per module so its effect is readable and revertible — P8b's "commit like a scientist" gate at fleet scale). Observe (reproduce + instrument + prime the workshop) and verify (re-run the primed repro) are not serial and carry the cycle's highest, most variable latency. So the throughput-optimal shape is a **prep-tier / act-tier split ("nurse/doctor")**: many parallel prep agents reproduce-and-prime workshops, thereby *producing the doctor's sole input: truthful, current-build, instrumented exam data* — reproduction is **production, not validation** (the bug report is a stale **sign**; the reproduced state is the **referent** — the per-bug instance of the theory's own language→ground-truth move, the doctor diagnoses from materialized state never the report's prose). The exam data is also a **triage oracle** that classifies the report: reproduces + engine-*wrong* → real bug → doctor; reproduces + engine-*right* → a **false bug** = a *communication defect on the author's side* (forgot-to-explain, or ambiguous phrasing, that induced a tester-side slip) → routed to the docs/phrasing owner, **not** the doctor (a cluster on one mechanic = fix the explanation at source, killing the recurring false-report stream — compounding); no-repro → discharged. Proving-the-bug-still-exists (no phantom fixes) and the apples-to-apples verify oracle (same agent reproduces and verifies) are *free byproducts* of a production step required regardless; and because the exam data is build-specific ground truth, it cannot be cached across a version cut (a reason for epoch re-prep, not caution); a thin serial acting tier (one agent per *disjoint lockable module*, not necessarily one global) visits primed workshops and performs only the settled mutation; the original prep agent verifies its own workshop; the module-scoped regression corpus remains the integrated ship-gate (local verify is necessary, not sufficient). *Ownership:* doctors bind to a **module** (affinity + lock); nurses are module-agnostic and each own **one bug + its exam data** end-to-end (reproduce → route → verify) — so affinity is quarantined to the thin serial tier and the scalable prep tier stays module-stateless (trivially scalable), routing is emergent (exam data names the module), and the verify oracle has a single custodian. Objective: drive the scarce serial slot's utilization → 1 (Theory-of-Constraints: exploit the constraint, subordinate the rest). Chief risk = **prep staleness** (an upstream fix invalidates a downstream primed workshop — cache invalidation). Preferred resolution (rkz): **batch by version epoch** — doctors work a run of fixes against a *frozen* build, then *close the office*: cut a new engine version and re-admit bugs only by re-prepping every workshop on the new build. This is **discard-and-rebuild, not invalidate-and-patch** — the workshop cache is made disposable rather than kept coherent, licensed by ~free parallel prep hours (spend the cheap resource to delete the hard coordination problem). One boundary solves three things at once: it voids cross-batch staleness wholesale, **amortizes the two shared-resource bottlenecks** (single deploy slot + full-corpus gate → once per epoch, not once per fix), and makes the re-prep pass a *free filter* that discharges side-effect-fixed bugs at the door. Cost is paid in the non-binding resource (per-bug ship-latency, not doctor throughput); the tuning knob shifts from prep-buffer WIP to **epoch size** (adaptive close: when still-reproducing queue < threshold, or a fix/time budget). Reuses the existing build-stamp version gate as the epoch boundary.

- **P8 — The workshop method (necessary discipline for tractable slips).** *From P1.* A slip (P1) is invisible in source (the source is symbols; the fault is a symbol→concept selection at run-time in a specific context). Therefore it is locatable only by **observation of the actual run**: reproduce in an instrumented clone — a **reversible perturbation sandbox**, not a static microscope — observe the realized data-flow, and — given a prior known-good state — deconstruct backward, proving which nodes still resolve correctly until the divergent node is isolated. Much of the "deconstruction" is *experimental* (perturb-and-read), not purely analytical, which is licensed by P8b. **No guessing from source.** **Observability is free at volume 1 — so max it out.** Total logging cost = per-event cost × event volume; production/discovery environments carry enormous volume (every step of every run) and *must* log thin for speed — not a shortcut but the only affordable setting at that volume. A reproduced case has **volume 1**, so per-event logging can go unbounded at trivial total cost: the workshop takes the **full-body MRI, not a targeted ultrasound** (no latency difference in software, unlike medicine). Reproduction is the **volume-collapse** that unlocks total observability; capturing everything up front makes diagnosis **single-pass** — the fixer "has it all" and never *orders a new scan*, deleting the add-a-log-and-re-run loop that dominates ordinary debugging (an observability instance of P8b's spend-the-free-resource move). **Caveat (necessary, not sufficient — the MRI kills missing-data errors, not misread errors).** Total observability removes only *couldn't-see-it*; it does nothing for *saw-it-and-read-one-house-over*. A fixer holding the complete scan can still diagnose the **symptom** and miss the **cause** — itself a P1 slip, now committed by the *debugger*. Observed live (2026-07-23) — a **three-level descent toward ground truth, each level refuting the last** (a bug reported as "L3 fabricated an NPC attack"): **(1)** the doctor's first instinct was a band-aid ("tell L3 no other creature attacked"), then a stronger guard — **"when an NPC holds, it does nothing"** — which would have scored the run *green* while making every held NPC's speech/intent *definitionally impossible* (a misdiagnosis promoted to a rule: worse than the bug, wearing the color of health). The human caught it (P9) by pointing at two words — the NPC *spoke* while holding. Pointing at the say moved diagnosis to the **right class** — L3 acting *unbound by L2's decided ground truth* ("missing the say/action") — and that class was correct. **(2)** The first *vector* guessed inside that class was "the decided `say` was dropped from the store, and L3 filled the vacuum" — adopted by the human and the analyst, and first recorded here. **(3)** The forced-branch reproduction *refined the vector without refuting the class*: the bug is **variance-dependent** (the natural repro rolled `action='attack'`, looked clean; the `hold+say` branch had to be *forced*), and the workshop dump showed **the say was present in the store** — so the mechanism was not a literal drop but the manifest *discarding the decided action* (`hold`) and **affirmatively inviting L3 to improvise** — "if its nature is to fight, it steps up and fights" — an improv-license that *overrode* the say. Same family (L3 unbound by the decided ground truth); the say was "missing" in the sense that *governed L3*, though present in the store. Lessons: (i) the MRI is necessary-not-sufficient — the doctor had the data and still misread (couldn't-see is gone; saw-and-misread is not), so **the concept-holder (P9) is not retired by total observability**; (ii) the human's class-level pointer was right, but pinning the exact **vector** still needed ground truth — a hypothesis at the right altitude is not yet the mechanism (P8: reproduce, don't theorize; and for variance-dependent bugs, *force the failing branch* — a clean natural repro proves nothing). Correct fix (truth-by-construction): hand L3 the decided action as ground truth **and remove the standing improv-invitation** — supplying the missing truth is not enough while a license to invent still competes with it. *(This is also the structural reason P6a holds in practice: an agent staring at the one stuck node fixes what it sees, resisting P3b.)*

- **P8b — Plasticity of the repair medium: mold, don't sculpt (certainty is back-loaded when reversal cost → 0).** *Economics + observation.* An AI-manipulated codebase is a **plastic** medium — changes apply *and* withdraw at fine granularity, near-instantly, with the live system's response immediately observable. This inverts the certainty economics of an **irreversible** medium (sculpture; a one-shot production change / migration): there, per-error cost is high, so certainty must be **front-loaded** (exhaustive analysis before a single act — "measure twice, cut once"). Here reversal cost ≈ 0, so certainty is **back-loaded** — reached by iteration: perturb, read the real result, keep what observed state confirms, revert the rest.
  - *(i) Over-analysis is a category error* imported from the irreversible medium: the live system is a cheaper, truer oracle than a model of it (cf. P8). Paying front-loaded-certainty costs on a self-healing material is waste.
  - *(ii) Finer than checkpoint rollback:* the operative capability is **selective retention of part of a change** (nudge, recombine, keep-half), not all-or-nothing revert per commit. Plasticity, not merely a safety net.
  - *(iii) It is what makes repair volume tractable:* low per-trial cost → high trial count (the ≈1,200 fixes on Thornwood were *made practical by* near-free reversal; throughput and this epistemics are one fact, cf. P7).
  - **Guardrail — plasticity requires a grounded retention gate, else it is thrash.** Reversibility makes *exploration* cheap; it does **not** license ungrounded *commitment*. The retention criterion must be observed **state** (was it actually fixed?), never **surface** ("looks fixed"). The band-aid reflex (P4) is exactly plasticity with the exploration test used as the retention test. **Rule: explore like a molder (fast, reversible, many probes); commit like a scientist (retain only what state-inspection confirms).** For a concept-holder reading state, plasticity is a superpower (molds toward truth); for a concept-blind agent reading surface, the *same* plasticity is a footgun (molds toward plausibility) — which is why P6 (reach-bound) and genuine-play/state-inspection are the gate that converts molding into sculpting-in-clay rather than thrash.

## 4. The human-necessity theory

- **P9 — The semantic catch needs a concept-holding director (human-required, not LLM-substitutable).** *From A5, P1.* Catching a semantic slip requires holding the invariant meaning (D8) fixed across the trajectory and diffing each node's realized selection against it. Per A3/D4 the LLM re-derives meaning per node and does not hold a trajectory-invariant; per A5 the director who built the system does. Therefore a second reader holding the invariant can identify the divergent node the original reader missed — **and, critically, only a reader whose errors are *decorrelated on the semantic class* can (a same-distribution LLM cannot; see the reformulation).** **This is a structural difference in what a *cross-reader* holds vs. what the acting agent holds, not a capability gap closed with scale.** *Scope: the residual language-space decisions (those not migrated by P5), i.e. genuine judgment/semantics.*

  > **Reformulation (peer review — decorrelation is by ERROR CLASS; the semantic catch is human-required).** P9 must not be read as "an *infallible* human is the terminal check" — a keeper can slip, hence "not immune." But the *opposite* flatten is the more dangerous one and is corrected here: **not just any independent reader decorrelates the misread.** Peer review splits by error class. **(i) Mechanical/structural errors** (internal inconsistency, dangling references, broken derivations) are decorrelated *even between LLMs* — an independent model linter catches them (a panel review of THIS document, 2026-07-23, correctly found a real internal contradiction in it). **(ii) The semantic misread** — the concept-gap slip itself (P1), e.g. "hold → does nothing" — is **positively correlated across LLMs**, because they share the training distribution and priors that *produce* the slip; a second model slips the same way and adds ≈0 bits (attempted on this project — it did not work). The only reader decorrelated from the acting LLM *on the semantic class* is a **human who holds the system's concepts** — the invariant, the design intent, "the point behind the communication." Crucially, that concept-holding is **installed by directing the build**, so the reader must be the **director of the work under review** ("with their own works"), not a fresh outside expert (who, however skilled, does not hold *this* system's invariant). **So A5 is NOT retired; it is required and precisely scoped:** the semantic catch depends on a **concept-holding director**. Its epistemic status is an *existence proof*, stated at exactly its width — neither narrower nor broader: **demonstrated n=1** (this director did it — a competent AI-literate IT professional, explicitly *not* claimed unique, the relevant experience largely earned in the doing, tied to the system by having authored it with the AI as hands), and **plausibly but unprovenly** fillable by other similar professionals, each on their own builds. No same-distribution LLM substitutes; no arbitrary human does either. This is a *decorrelation* claim, not an infallibility one (the director slips too and benefits from further review, including of his own reads). **Replication warning — the single most easily glossed thing here, and it misleads in BOTH directions:** *understate* it (the loop self-corrects / any reviewer or another LLM suffices) and a replicator fails and perceives **fraud where there is none**; *overstate* it (a lone genius) and it reads as an unrepeatable stunt. The calibrated truth is narrow *and* repeatable: a concept-holding director reviewing their own build — the *skill* is a fillable role, not a unicorn; what is genuinely scarce is not ability but the **conviction to run it unproven and the stamina to stay present at every step** (§8). (Live 2026-07-23: the director caught the debugger's "hold → does nothing" misread with a two-word delta — P10; the LLM panel run to "get a second opinion" on this very document caught only the mechanical class and **could not itself produce this correction** — the concept-holding director did.)

- **P10 — The unblock is delta-injection, not dialogue.** *From D9.* A stuck agent (by definition out of productive moves) cannot converge in a dialogue; conversation degrades to observed band-aiding (P4). The efficient intervention transmits **only the delta** (D9): the missing concept, conditional on what the agent already holds. Confirming correct behavior carries 0 information (redundant); explaining derivable steps carries negative value (consumes scarce human bandwidth). The minimal encoding of the delta exploits shared context as a shared codebook (high context → few symbols per concept), i.e. **expert shorthand** ("commander→XO"). *The standing plan the human deviates from = the corpus/architecture the agent already executes correctly.*

- **P11 — The oversight channel is asymmetric (eyes-in, voice-out).** *From P9, P10, and human perceptual/productive asymmetry.* Optimize each direction for its recipient:
  - **agent→human:** dense, structured, **visual, random-access** (tables/diagrams/layout), delivered complete (not streamed), so the human locates the delta (P9) by parallel perception and re-scan. A table's value over prose is a bandwidth claim (parallel vs serial intake).
  - **human→agent:** **voice** (production bandwidth > typing).
  - In this system, symmetric typed chat *appeared* dominated on both ends, and voice-both-ways worse agent→human (destroys re-scan); the interface *converged on* **report-and-command, not conversation** — the structural inverse of consumer conversational AI. *(Observed preference, uncontrolled n=1; P11 is flagged least-precedented and priority-to-test — F-P11.)* *Design corollary: the briefing must expose the agent's **frontier** (its model, its ruled-outs, its stuck-node), so the human can diff-and-locate in one scan.*

## 5. Synthesis (dependency chain)

```
A1 (language ≠ concepts) ─┬─> P1 (slip) ─┬─> P2 (union bound: capability ≠ reliability)
A2 (correlation)          │              ├─> P3a (depth failure)
A3 (LLM in language-space)┘              └─> P9 (concept-holding DIRECTOR = decorrelated semantic reader) ─> P10 (delta) ─> P11 (channel)
A5 (director holds the invariant) ───────> P9   [semantic catch: human-required; LLM catches only mechanical class]
A4 (code = concept-space) ───────────────> P5 (concept-space cure = migrate the decision OR check the output; both on DECIDABLE classes)
P1 ──────────────────────> P4 (guard-wall grows without bound: economic, not impossibility) ─> motivates P5
(repair observation) ────> P3b (overreach) ─> P6 (reach-bound + inverted-U) ─> P7 (parallel/isolation)
P1 ─────────────────────> P8 (workshop: observe, don't guess)
P9 (single invariant-keeper) ⟂ P7 (parallel fleet) ──> OPEN: serial-attention/reload ceiling (§8)
```

**One-line theory.** An LLM has language, not concepts (A1–A3); so it fails by pointwise re-interpretation (P1) at a rate capability cannot zero (P2); the terminating repair is to move decisions into concept-space where re-interpretation is impossible (P5), because corrections made of language never converge (P4); the residual language-space decisions require a **concept-holding director** (not another same-distribution LLM, which shares the slip) to catch the semantic misreads by holding the invariant across the trajectory (P9 — an existence proof, n=1, plausibly fillable by peers on their own builds), interacting by minimal delta-injection (P10) over an asymmetric visual-in/voice-out channel (P11), while the repair agents themselves must be bounded in reach (P6) and parallelized via isolation (P7).

---

## 6. Prior-art placement

- **P1/A1** are the symbol-grounding problem and the map–territory / sense–reference distinction, specialized to LLM decision nodes and made *operational* (a locatable, reproducible fault) rather than philosophical.
- **P2** is a union-bound reliability argument; its content is that the relevant variable is jurisdiction/node-count, not model quality.
- **P5** is "LLM proposes, code disposes" / neuro-symbolic division of labor, generalized to *two* terminating concept-space cures (migrate the decision, or deterministically check the output) available on the same **decidable** classes; the undecidable residue is P9's domain.
- **P6b** (effort inverted-U for repair) and **P11** (asymmetric report-and-command channel) are, to the author's knowledge, the least-precedented claims and the priority ones to test.

---

## 7. Falsifiers (what would refute each claim)

- **F-P2:** a model whose scaling drives production slip-rate to ~0 on complex trajectories **without** transferring decisions out of language-space. Would refute the union-bound framing.
- **F-P4:** a purely language-level correction regime (rules/prompts, no jurisdiction transfer) that reaches a **bounded, non-growing** correction set at steady state while holding a slip class closed on a complex system. Would refute the guard-wall-grows-without-bound (economic) claim.
- **F-P5:** a case where migrating a decision to deterministic code does **not** remove its slip class (i.e. code that "re-interprets"). Would refute the mechanism.
- **F-P6b:** repair quality found **monotone** in reasoning effort (max ≥ medium) on interwoven systems, under controlled comparison. Would refute the inverted-U.
- **F-P9:** a same-distribution LLM (or LLM panel) reliably catching the *semantic-misread* class — the concept-gap slips (P1) — at concept-holding-director parity, **without** a human supplying the invariant. Would refute the human-required form of P9/A5. (Current evidence runs the other way: an LLM second-opinion on this project caught only the mechanical class and missed the semantic catch — 2026-07-23.)
- **F-P11:** symmetric conversational or voice-both-ways interfaces measured to match/beat visual-in/voice-out for expert throughput on the approval task. Would refute the channel asymmetry.

## 8. Scope, limits, and the caveat that matters

- **Two denominators — "n = 1" applies to only one of them.** The evidence base has two independent sizes, and conflating them is itself a Chapter-2 slip (reading the sign "n=1" one house past the concept). *(a) Generalization axis:* one system (Thornwood), one architecture, one domain, ~one model generation → here the sample genuinely **is** n=1, and no amount of hours widens it; it bounds which claims transfer (all the "will this hold elsewhere / for future architectures" claims). *(b) Per-decision-phenomenon axis:* the *every-decision* mechanisms (P1 slip / band-aid reflex, P10 delta-correction) were observed across the project's **~2,000–4,000 discrete direction decisions** — *estimated from a measured base, not guessed wholesale*: 38 distinct active commit-days and 1,610 commits are git ground truth (2026-05-21→07-23, *not* the 63-day calendar span); the 12–18 h/day, the ≈**456–684 direction-hours** (≈2.6–4.0 full-time-months), and the decision count are estimated on that base, cross-checked by commit cadence (~1 commit / 17–25 min). On this axis N is in the **thousands of trials**, so those phenomenon-claims deserve more confidence than "case study" implies. **Caveat:** this denominator covers only the *every-decision* phenomena; P6/P6b (repair-effort) manifest only on the *small subset* of hard repair runs and do **not** inherit it (see below). **Rule:** state the denominator that matches the claim — generalization claims carry n=1; every-decision phenomenon claims carry n≈10³·⁵; repair-effort claims carry the small hard-run count.
- **A5 is the honest, replication-critical requirement — and the most easily glossed, in BOTH directions.** *Under-claim* (harmful): "the loop self-corrects" / "any independent reader or another LLM catches the misread" — false (LLMs share priors → correlated on the semantic class; demonstrated, an LLM second-opinion "did not work" there) and it invites replication that fails and reads as fraud. *Over-claim* (also harmful): "it takes a unique genius" — the director is *not* a talent-unicorn and the *skill* is fillable. But the honest account must name what actually WAS scarce, and it is **not ability — it is disposition and stamina**: the confidence/stubbornness to run an unproven method to completion with no guarantee, the self-trust to hold that bet through the long uncertain middle, and the diligence to be personally present at every commit and all ~1,200 fixes (concept-holding cannot be delegated or made intermittent) — a sustained comprehension load the director likens to *force-reading a large, uncounted number of books* cover to cover (the eyes-in cost of P11 paid in full: the keeper is fundamentally a reader). This was demanding *even for* someone well-suited. So the practical replication gate is **grit/conviction, not talent** — many have the skill; fewer will stake conviction on the unproven thing and grind every step personally. A steps-only write-up erases this — the hardest, realest ingredient, and the one nowhere in the transcript. The calibrated claim is an **existence proof of exactly this width**: the semantic catch requires a **concept-holding director** — a competent AI-literate IT professional reviewing *their own* build (concept-holding is installed by directing it) — demonstrated once here, and *plausibly but unprovenly* fillable by similar professionals on their own works. This is what §8.2 of the experience report means by "expert-directed"; it is stated at this width because both narrower and broader readings mislead. A *mechanism* (not a same-distribution LLM) shown to hold and enforce a trajectory-invariant with decorrelated error would weaken P9 to "*some* decorrelated concept-holder is required" — not shown; an LLM is not it.
- **Degree vs kind.** A critic may hold that concepts are merely very-stable language, making the strong claim (A3-strong: the model has *no* concepts, only language) a difference of degree rather than kind. **The theory concedes this and does not need to win it** (see Remark after P2): the load-bearing observable is that the slip (P1) is *simultaneously* small enough to trust ("forgivable language-wise") and large enough to be fatal ("fatal in practice"). That a single symbol can be *close enough to rely on and wrong enough to crash* is all P2–P11 consume — an operational fact about reliability, not a metaphysical proof that pointer and referent are distinct kinds. The degree-vs-kind question is left genuinely open; the engineering is indifferent to its answer.
- **P6/P6b rest on uncontrolled observation.** The effort inverted-U (P6b) and the reach-bound finding (P6) are impressions from a small number of hard repair runs, not a controlled measurement — reported as observed-and-acted-on, not established. F-P6b is the experiment that would settle it. Treat P6b as the theory's most empirically fragile proposition after A5.
- **OPEN PROBLEM — P9 ⟂ P7 (the single keeper vs the parallel fleet).** P9 requires a *fully-loaded* concept-holder (the delta is minimal *because* the human already holds the whole trajectory-state, D9/P10). P7 asks one such holder to oversee `M` concurrent isolated trajectories. One head cannot hold `M` full invariants simultaneously; the two propositions are in tension. **Partial resolution + prediction:** the *fleet* is concurrent but the *human's attention is serial and event-driven* — engaged one room at a time, on exception. This relocates the binding cost from bandwidth-per-glance to **state-reload per interruption** (paging one trajectory's full invariant back into a single head). Consequence: the eyes-in channel (P11) is not for watching `M` rooms at once (impossible) but for making reload cheap. **Prediction (F-candidate):** the ceiling on fleet size `M` is set by the human's context-switch/reload rate without thread-loss, **not** by compute or agent count; beyond some `M*`, added agents raise defect-throughput sublinearly and then negatively as reload thrash dominates. Unverified; the sharpest open edge of the theory.
- **Reflexive caveat.** This document is itself language (D2) and was co-produced by an LLM (D4). Whether stable concepts underlie its symbols or only well-fitted language is precisely the open question it formalizes; §7's falsifiers, not its prose, are what should be trusted.
